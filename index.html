About childhood cancer...

The individual cause of more than 90% of childhood cancer cases remains unknown.

During the period 2011-2015, an average of 714 new cancers were diagnosed each year among children aged 0-14 years, 16 for every 100,000 children.

The incidence of cancer in children aged 0 to 4 is almost twice that of children aged 5 to 9, and 1.6 times that of children aged 10 to 14.

Between 2015 and 2017, 275 children aged 0 to 14 died of cancer. On average, 92 people die each year, or 2 per 100,000.

Australia is estimated to have the fifth highest incidence of childhood cancer among G20 countries, just behind Germany, the United States, South Korea and Canada.
